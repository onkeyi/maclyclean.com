The Problem

Your Mac is hiding gigabytes of clutter you can't see

macOS has no built-in deep cleaner. Files accumulate silently in hidden system folders, eating your storage without a single notification.

📁

Hidden System Caches

Every app you use — from Safari to Xcode — writes cache data to hidden system folders. These folders grow silently in the background. A freshly-used Mac can accumulate several gigabytes of cache files within weeks, and macOS provides no automated tool to clear them safely.

📄

Duplicate Files Everywhere

Downloaded the same installer twice? Exported a Figma design into multiple folders? Backed up photos manually? Identical files scatter across your drive — occupying double (or triple) the necessary space, with no easy way to find them.

💿

Forgotten DMG Installers

Every app installed from a .dmg disk image leaves that file sitting on your drive after installation. Most users never delete them. A typical .dmg ranges from 100 MB to several gigabytes, and over a few years of installing apps, these forgotten files can consume tens of gigabytes of your storage.

🔨

Developer Tool Bloat

Build tools and package managers each keep their own caches and downloaded data indefinitely. Combined, developer tool caches routinely exceed 20–50 GB — even on machines used only occasionally for development.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is Smart Scan really free forever?
Yes — no catch, no expiry, no account. Smart Scan (the Junk Files module) is completely free with no time limit. It targets system caches and logs — the most impactful sources of recoverable disk space on a Mac. We believe every Mac user should be able to clear basic junk for free, which is why Smart Scan is free in every version of Macly, forever.
What does the 7-day free trial include?
The free trial unlocks all 11 cleaning modules — Duplicates, App Uninstaller, Photos, Mail, DMG Files, Trash, Dev Caches, Screenshots, and Browser Cache — for 7 days at no cost and with no credit card required. After the trial expires, Smart Scan remains free and you can purchase a license at any time to restore full access to all modules.
Is Macly safe to use on my Mac?
Macly always shows you exactly what it found — with file names and sizes — before deleting anything. You select what to remove, then confirm. Five modules (Mail, Screenshots, DMG, Dev Caches, Browser Cache) move files to macOS Trash so you can restore anything afterward. Junk Files are permanently deleted because macOS regenerates them automatically — they cannot contain personal data. Photos deletions are permanent, which is why Macly includes a full preview feature for every photo and video before you select it.
Which macOS versions does Macly support?
Macly requires macOS 13 Ventura or later. It is a universal binary that runs natively on Apple Silicon Macs (M1, M2, M3, M4) and Intel Macs. macOS 12 Monterey and earlier are not supported.
How does the one-time license work?
After purchasing through Lemon Squeezy, a license key is delivered to your email address. Open Macly, go to Settings, and enter the key — it is stored securely in macOS Keychain, so you only need to enter it once per Mac. A Personal license covers 1 Mac, Family covers up to 3, and Team covers up to 10. There is no subscription, no annual renewal, and no expiry date. All future updates are included at no additional cost.
Why does the Mail module require Full Disk Access?
Apple Mail stores all attachments in a folder that macOS protects through its privacy framework. Full Disk Access is the permission that allows Macly to read this protected area. You grant it once in System Settings, and Macly only reads file names and sizes — it never uploads, transmits, or reads the content of your emails or attachments.
Can I restore files after cleaning?
Yes — for most modules. Mail, Screenshots, DMG Files, Dev Caches, and Browser Cache all move files to macOS Trash rather than permanently deleting them. After every cleanup, Macly displays a restore list on the completion screen with a per-item Restore button and a Restore All button. You can also open Finder → Trash and drag files back manually. Junk Files (caches/logs), Photos, Trash contents, and Duplicates are permanently deleted and cannot be restored.
